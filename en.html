Swiss Travel Pass Check

How to use Swiss Travel Pass Check (click to expand)
1) Enter travelers
- Start by entering the number of Adults (25+), Youth (16–25), Children (6–15), and Infants (0–5).
2) Select season (Summer/Winter)
- Pick a travel date on the calendar and the season is determined automatically.
- If your trip is on the boundary, you can lock the season using the dropdown.
3) Select passes/tickets
- Select the passes you plan to use.
4) Select regions/routes you’ll actually take
- On the right, expand a region and check the routes you will take.
5) Enter prices
- First choose the “Price mode (Baseline/Deal)”. Baseline = regular fare. Deal = Saver/Super Saver (may exclude certain trains/times).
- Click “SBB One-click” to search that segment directly on SBB.
- After selecting a date, check the price for your preferred time, then enter it here.
- Enter fares considering all travelers (Adults/Youth/Children), and account for one-way vs round-trip when selecting routes.
- If you catch a Super Saver deal, you’re a lucky guy⭐
- Routes included in your selected pass are automatically set to CHF 0 and locked, so you don’t need to enter anything.
6) Review results
- On the left, check the total “Passes/Tickets + Routes”.
- In the breakdown, you can see what was counted as free/discounted.
7) Category legend
public - public transport (trains/buses)
boat - lake boats / cruises
scenic - panoramic trains / scenic routes
mountain - mountain railways / cable cars
special - extra fee applies
Notice (click to expand)
- VIP included routes can vary by season (Summer/Winter) and may change due to operations/maintenance.
- Some panoramic trains may require an additional seat reservation fee.
- Prices/discount rules may change depending on timing and conditions.
